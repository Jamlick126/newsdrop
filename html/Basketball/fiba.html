2023 FIBA WORLD CHAMPIONS!

10 SEP 2023 6 minutes read

Germany are FIBA World Cup champions for the first time in history. Their undefeated run through the 2023 FIBA World Cup including an upset in the semifinal win over the United States ended with an 83-77 victory over Serbia in the gold medal game.

Serbia has now lost in two of the last three World Cups in the gold medal game having fallen to the United States in 2014. The last medal Germany took home was a bronze medal in 2002 with Dirk Nowitzki leading the way.

It was a team effort as Dennis Schröder who signed with the Toronto Raptors this offseason led the way for the victors with 28 points and Franz Wagner doing a little bit of everything with 19 points, 7 rebounds, 3 steals and 2 assists.

Wagner led the way in the first half with his scoring but the game headed into intermission tied. Schröder went into takeover mode for extended stretches in the third quarter and gave Germany a comfortable 12-point lead heading into the fourth. Serbia showed some heart as it came charging back in the forth quarter with Aleksa Avramović (21 points) and Bogdan Bogdanović (17 points and 5 assists) leading the way.

But the difference in the game was the Raptor's newly signed point guard. When the game was on the line, the NBA veteran blew past his defender and finished at the rim in the final 30 seconds to push the advantage back to four points and a two possession game drawing plenty of praise for his clutch showcase.

It's Unbelievable! Going 8-0 and winning the gold medal. That's a blessing to share that with all the people in the locker room, my family who came all the way from Germany, my kids, my wife. It's unbelievable, man!

His layup was the most important shot of the tournament and he iced the victory on the free-throw line. It was a performance that will go down as one the best in Germany basketball history as he delivered in the biggest moment.
