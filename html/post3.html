Community Shield

August 7th 2023 5 min read

The Community Shield played at Wembly was a contest between Champions(Man City) and the runners up Arsenal since City also won the FA Cup. The Gunners barely got a kick in the opening 30 minutes as City made full use of their overload in the build-up, but it was Mikel Arteta’s side, who grew into the contest considerably after the half-hour mark, that spurned the two best chances of the first half. Kai Havertz was twice denied by Stefan Ortega.

The game evolved into an exciting back-and-forth contest after the break as both teams enjoyed periods of superiority. No surprise it was last season’s treble winners who landed the opening blow as substitute Cole Palmer curled home superbly with 15 minutes remaining. That looked like the winner, but the Gunners struck right at the death through Trossard, whose shot deflected off Akanji bypassing Ortega deep into stoppage time. The Belgian’s strike took the contest straight to penalties. Arsenal's defence led by William Saliba kept the lethal Man City striker Eirling Halaand quiet through out the game with very little service since their star playmaker Kevin De Bruyne was on the bench.

Arsenal prevailed and claimed victory on penalties after Kevin De Bruyne and Rodri missed their spot kicks. This victory might give the Gunners confidence in staging a title charge this season.
