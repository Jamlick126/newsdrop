"The Joker" delivers!

July 15th 2023 5 min read

Denver Nuggets Allstar Nikola Jokic was named NBA Finals MVP after leading his team to their first NBA title over Miami Heat 94-89 in Game 5. Prior to this championship run, Jokic, 28, was already considered one of the best players in the league. But now tha he has won a championship and a Finals MVP, this catapults the Serbian big man into a different stratosphere of the best palyer on the planet.

Jokic joins Antetokounmpo, Dirk Nowitzki, Tony Paker, Tim Duncan and Hakeem Olajuwon as the only players born overseas to take home the Finals award. And he did it by putting together one of the all-time great post-seasons runs. He had 10 tripple-doubles during his run, with eight in a 12 game span. He became the first player to have a 30-20-10 trpple-double in Finals History with 32 points, 21 rebounds and 10 assists in Game 3 at Miami.

" I think it's a great journey, like you said, 41st pick but that doesn't matter. When you are here, you're an NBA player." Jokic said about going from a second-round pick to Finals MVP.

Jokic is the lowest-drafted player(41st overall in the second round in 2014) to win the Finals MVP, passing Dennis Johnson, who was drafted 29th overall in 1979, according to ESPN Stats and Information.
